Reserve Policies

The Reserve system allows faculty to set aside books, media, articles, and other materials for the use of students enrolled in a class. The Reserve request system has changed, and all reserves requests will now be submitted through ILLiad on the library website.

Please keep the following policies in mind:

  • Requests are processed in the order received.
  • Incomplete forms will likely cause delays.
  • We recommend submitting requests at least three (3) weeks prior to the beginning of the semester.
  • If the items are not in the University Libraries' collections and need to be ordered, give extra time.
  • e-Reserve requests will generate a confirmation email, but requests that books and media be put on physical reserves will not generate a confirmation email.
  • If you need to put a personal copy of an item on reserve, note this on the form.
  • You will make a separate request on ILLiad for each item you put on reserve.
  • Requests must comply with current copyright laws and University intellectual property policies.

Two types of Reserves Requests

There are two types of reserve requests:

  • e-Reserves: the library will digitize articles, chapters, and selections from books. You will be provided a link to digital copies of these e-Reserves which your students will be able to access through Blackboard. You no longer need to provide copies for e-Reserves.
  • Book and Media reserves: Physical copies placed of books and media placed on Reserve may be checked by students for short periods from designated libraries. You may put personal copies on Reserve.

Getting to ILLiad, the e-Reserves and Reserves Request System

The Reserve request system has changed, and all reserves requests will now be submitted through ILLiad on the library website. Before placing e-Reserves or Book and Media Reserves Requests, you will need to access ILLiad. To do so, follow these steps.

  1. To access ILLiad, the Reserves Request System, navigate to the library's homepage at library.gmu.edu. Then, select 'Borrow & Renew' from the My Library section of the homepage.

  2. Select 'My Library Accounts.' This page enables you to access your library records, including ILLiad.

  3. Select 'My ILLiad Account'. This is the same page that you use to request items through the Inter Library Loan and Inter Campus Loan services.

  4. Log in using your Mason NetID and password. Your Mason NetID is the same as the portion of your Mason email address preceding the @ sign.

  5. If this is your first time using ILLiad you will need to complete the Add/Change Personal Information form. You may edit your personal information later from within the ILLiad site.

  6. Now, you are ready to submit e-Reserves and Course reserves requests. You will be able to submit more than one e-Reserves or Course Reserves request per login.

Placing an e-Reserves Request

The e-Reserves system now delivers articles, selections of books, or other shorter materials electronically to your students inside Blackboard. This replaces the older system which required a separate login to access e-Reserves. To place an e-Reserves request, follow these steps.

  1. Select the 'Reserve Article or Book Chapter' option from the menu on the left. While instructors were asked to provide copies of articles or book chapters for reserves in the past, the Library reserves staff are now responsible for locating and scanning your articles and book chapters.

  2. Complete the request form as fully as possible. Please note that * indicates a required field.

Help

Placing Book and Media Reserve Requests

Books and Media Reserves allow you to place items on reserve inside the library. This enables you to limit the checkout period for items so that all students in a class may use them. You may reserve items from the library holdings or put your own personal items on reserve. To place a book and media reserves request, follow these steps.

  1. Choose 'Reserve Book or Media Item' from the left hand menu while in ILLiad.The Library reserves staff will locate the book or media item you place on reserve for you in the library holdings. If you need to place a personal copy of a book or media item on reserve for your class, you will note this on the form and deliver the item to a library location.

  2. Complete the Request Form as fully as possible. Please note that * indicates a required field.

  3. After completing the form, choose the 'Submit Request' button at the bottom of the form. This will complete your request.

  4. You will need to submit a request for each item that you wish to place on reserve. If you have more items to request, you may request them now.

  5. Only e-Reserve requests will generate a confirmation email. Do not worry if you do not hear back. You may check the the library catalog to see if the book is placed on reserve.
