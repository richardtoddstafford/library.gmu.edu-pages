Introduction

The Washington Research Library Consortium (WRLC) is a service where patrons may borrow books, articles, and other materials not available in the George Mason University Library system from a group of regional university libraries:

  • American University
  • Catholic University
  • Gallaudet University
  • Georgetown University
  • George Washington University
  • Howard University
  • Marymount University
  • University of D.C.

  1. Select ‘Borrow & Renew’ in the upper right hand corner of the library’s homepage at library.gmu.edu

  2. Select ‘My Library Accounts’

  3. Select ‘WRLC catalog’ under ‘My WRLC Library Account’

  4. Search for the item needed, using the drop down menus to refine and limit your search to field (title, author, etc.) and/or library (American, Catholic, etc.).

  5. Select the item needed from the results list, if the item needed is not found, make an ILL Request

  6. Select the ‘Request’ button in the upper right hand corner. (The ‘request’ button will take you to the WRLC login page.)

  7. Login with your last name, university ID (either Mason G# or Mason ID barcode), and institution.

  8. Complete the appropriate form, please remember to select the appropriate library location and reenter your university ID (either Mason G# or Mason ID barcode)

  9. Select the ‘Submit Request’ button at the bottom of the form.

Help

Additional Information

  • You will be notified via email when your request is available.
  • Articles will be delivered online to your WRLC account. Access them using the ‘Web Docs’ link under ‘My Library Account’ in the upper left hand corner of the page.
  • Books and other materials will be delivered to the selected library location.
  • If you have trouble placing a request or have specific questions about your request, contact a library service desk:
    • Fenwick: 703.993.2240
    • Arlington: 703.993.8188
    • Prince William: 703.993.8340
  • The average time to receive books, articles, etc. is two (2) or three (3) days. If you need items faster, you can borrow them directly from the WRLC university libraries with your Mason ID.
  • If the WRLC does not have the item you need, or if you're a distance education (DE) student, request your material through interlibrary loan (ILL).
